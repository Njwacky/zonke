## 📜 Section 1: Cultural Origin & Overview

**"Zonke"** (also affectionately known in South African townships as *Sojahs*, *Maphepha*, or *Kasi Wars*) is a traditional hand-drawn street and classroom game played across South Africa. Using torn out notebook paper, ballpoint pens, and tightly crumpled paper balls, players construct competing stick-figure armies row by row. 

In this digital HTML5 adaptation, two players compete head-to-head across a **12-Row Battlefield**, aiming flicks from a dedicated **Launch Pad (`🏀`)**, switching between **4 Custom Themes** and **4 Custom Character Outfits** via the **Hamburger Menu (`☰ MENU`)**, upgrading their armies via the **Center Bonus Arena (`--⭕--`)**, and engaging in cutthroat **Shoot First vs Dead (`💀😭` vs `👑 GOAT 🐐🤣`)** duels!

## ⚔️ Section 2: Complete Game Rules & Controls

### 1. The 12-Soldier Army (`Row 1 to Row 12`)
Each player commands an army of 12 soldiers divided across 12 individual rows (`Row 1 to Row 6` in the upper sector, `Row 7 to Row 12` in the lower sector). Every row operates as an independent soldier being built from scratch.

### 2. The Strict 12-Stage Construction Sequence
To fully assemble and arm a soldier on Row $i$, a player must land the paper ball onto Row $i$ exactly **12 times** in strict sequential order:
1. **Head** 🟡 *(Circle)*
2. **Neck** ┃ *(Vertical Spine)*
3. **Body** ┃ *(Upper Torso)*
4. **Arms** ━ *(Horizontal Stance)*
5. **Lower Body** ┃ *(Hips/Spine)*
6. **Legs** ∧ *(Bipedal Stance)*
7. **Gun** 🔫 *(Rifle Assembly)*
8. **Bullet 1** 🔴 *(Ammunition 1)*
9. **Bullet 2** 🔴 *(Ammunition 2)*
10. **Bullet 3** 🔴 *(Ammunition 3)*
11. **Bullet 4** 🔴 *(Ammunition 4)*
12. **Bullet 5 (Complete & Shoot 1st!)** 🔥 *(Final Ammunition — Soldier Ready to Fire!)*

### 3. Dedicated Launch Pad & Horizontal Power Bar (`🏀 LAUNCH`)
- To prevent visual overlap on the bottom target rows (`Row 11 & Row 12`), the paper ball rests inside its own **Launch Pad Cradle (`🏀 LAUNCH`)** at the bottom of the board.
- **Horizontal Power Bar:** Positioned right beside the launch cradle is the **Horizontal Power Bar (`[PWR ===|===]`)**. This ergonomic placement keeps your eyes focused directly on the bottom command zone (the ball, the oscillating timing marker, and the flick button below).
- **How to Launch:** Watch the oscillating horizontal marker moving continuously between $0\%$ and $100\%$ width. Click **`FLICK! [SPACE]`** (or press `Spacebar` / `Enter`).
- The ball launches vertically into the target zone, ricocheting across top and bottom walls (`damping = 0.82`) until gravity and multi-bounce physics slow it down enough to snap cleanly onto a target row!

### 4. The Center Bonus Arena (`--⭕--` / `--()--` Line)
Right across the dividing boundary between Row 6 and Row 7 (`Row Index 6/7 border`) lies the floating **BONUS ROUND (`--⭕-- BONUS +1 --⭕--`)**.
- **The Jackpot Effect:** Landing on this center target does not just advance one soldier. Instead, **EVERY SINGLE ALIVE INCOMPLETE SOLDIER IN YOUR ARMY (+1 to all surviving rows!)** instantly receives their next sequential stage!
- If multiple soldiers reach Stage 12 simultaneously during a bonus hit, your army unleashes a simultaneous volley of gunfire across the board!

### 5. Shoot First or Die (`👑 GOAT 🐐🤣` vs `💀 DEAD 😭`)
In true Kasi street fashion, armies do not build in peaceful isolation. Every row is a high-stakes duel between opposing soldiers:
- **First to Fire (`👑 GOAT 🐐🤣`):** The moment your soldier reaches Stage 12 (`Bullet 5`), your soldier is complete and **FIRES FIRST across the battlefield!**
- **Elimination (`💀 DEAD 😭`):** Your opponent's soldier across on that exact same row is instantly **SHOT DEAD AND ELIMINATED!** Their box turns pale crimson (`#ffebe9`), their stick figure grays out (`filter: grayscale(1)`), and they receive the **`💀 DEAD 😭`** stamp (`-1` status).
- **Permanence:** Once a soldier is dead (`-1`), that player can **never continue building or using that row**! Landing on a dead row wastes your turn.

### 6. The Majority Victory Condition (`First to 7 GOATs Wins!`)
Because every row completed by one player permanently kills the opponent's row, exactly 12 total soldiers can be built across the match.
- **The Win Rule:** The first player to complete **7 GOAT Soldiers (`7 out of 12 rows`)** holds an unbeatable majority (`7 vs at most 5`) and immediately **WINS THE KASI WAR (`THE GOAT 🐐🏆`)!**

## 🧠 Section 3: The 4 Whys (`4whys`) of Kasi Game Design

Why is Zonke structured with these exact rules, mechanics, and visual loops? Here are the **4 Core Philosophical "Whys"** (`4whys`) behind the game design:

```
+---------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                       THE 4 WHYS OF ZONKE GAME DESIGN                           |
+---------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|  1. WHY 12 ROWS & 12 STAGES?      --> Progression Granularity & Tangible Reward |
|  2. WHY THE CENTER BONUS `--⭕--`?  --> Comeback Mechanics & High-Stakes Jackpot  |
|  3. WHY SHOOT 1st OR DIE (`💀`)?  --> Cutthroat Interaction & Strategic Pivoting|
|  4. WHY DEDICATED LAUNCH PAD (`🏀`)?--> UI Ergonomics & Unobstructed Targeting  |
+---------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

### ❓ Why #1: Why 9 Rows and 12 Stages per Soldier (`108 Total Stages`)?
**Answer: Maximized Cell Geometry and Tangible Reward Curves.**
If soldiers only required 3 or 4 hits to build, matches would end in seconds and luck would overpower skill. By breaking each soldier into exactly **12 distinct anatomical parts (`Head -> Neck -> Torso -> Arms -> Hips -> Legs -> Rifle -> 5 Bullets`)** across exactly **9 distinct rows (`108 total parts per army`)**, every soldier box (`.soldier-box`) gains **`+33% vertical height`** ($46.7	ext{--}50	ext{px}$ tall). This allows our tightly-cropped chibi characters (`scale(1.44)`) to render boldly and clearly without vertical crushing, while the 12-stage depth rewards consistent power-bar timing over high-stakes duels!

---

### ❓ Why #2: Why the Center Bonus Circle (`--⭕-- +1`) & 1st Approach Horizontal Envelope?
**Answer: Comeback Mechanics and High-Stakes Jackpot Turnarounds.**
In any competitive accuracy game, when one player pulls ahead by 2 or 3 rows, the trailing player can experience "hopelessness fatigue." The center **`--⭕-- +1` Bonus Arena** solves this by inserting a high-risk, high-reward target directly on the dividing boundary between Row 5 and Row 6. 
By utilizing our reliable **1st approach horizontal envelope (`42% to 58% relativeBallY`)**, any settled or turning ball inside that center envelope reliably awards `ball.targetBoardRowIndex = 'BONUS'`. Because the bonus circle adds **`+1 stage to ALL alive soldiers at once`**, a trailing player who masterfully snipes the center target can upgrade up to 9 soldiers in a single turn (`+9 total stage value in one flick!`). This guarantees that no match is ever truly over until the 5th GOAT (`5/9 majority`) is crowned!

---

### ❓ Why #3: Why "Shoot First or Die" (`👑 GOAT 🐐🤣` vs `💀 DEAD 😭`) & Tinfoil Revive Fresh Restarts?
**Answer: Cutthroat Interaction, Strategic Risk Management, and True Comebacks.**
Without head-to-head row elimination, Zonke would merely be two parallel single-player bingo games. By enforcing **"Shoot 1st or Die (`💀 DEAD 😭`)"**, every row becomes a live duel where being second place equals permanent elimination (`-1`).
However, permanent death without counterplay creates snowballing lockouts. That is why we engineered the **`🪙 TINFOIL REVIVE SHOT (1 per match)` with Fresh Line Restarts (`both players starting fresh in that line`)**. When a reviving player strikes an eliminated row (`-1`), **both players reset to Stage 1 (`Head`) on that exact row!** This breaks the opponent's previous Stage 12 GOAT dominance and turns that row back into a brand new, live 1v1 race!

---

### ❓ Why #4: Why the Dedicated Launch Pad (`🏀 SWAP ⇄`), Horizontal Power Bar, & RGB Speed Circle?
**Answer: UI/UX Ergonomics, Air Drift Mastery, and Command Zone Focus.**
When adapting a physical street game, layout friction can ruin the experience. If the resting paper ball sat on top of `Row 8 & Row 9`, or if the power bar was placed vertically in the top-right corner, players' eyes would be forced to constantly dart back and forth across the screen while targeting.
By placing the **Resting Cradle (`🏀 SWAP ⇄`)**, the **Horizontal Power Bar (`[PWR ===|===]`)**, and our **RGB Speed Circle (`[ M ] / [ L ] / [ H ]`)** together side by side inside the dedicated bottom Launch Pad directly above the `FLICK!` button, the game creates an intuitive **"Bottom Command Zone."** All launching inputs, power timing, and manual left/right launch side repositioning (`to curve vertical shots with wind drift into the center bonus!`) originate from the exact same focal point, while all 9 target rows remain 100% unobstructed!

## 📱 Section 6: Capacitor + Node.js (`Socket.io`) Hybrid Multiplayer & Native Haptics

Zonke is engineered to run seamlessly across both **Offline Native Mobile (`Capacitor`)** and **Online Real-Time PvP (`Node.js + Socket.io`)** environments:
1. **📴 Offline Local Pass-and-Play Mode:** Compiled directly to native iOS (`Xcode .ipa`) and Android (`Android Studio .aab`) via `Capacitor`. When offline or in local mode, both players share the physical device. All 12 rows, 4 themes, 4 Mini Militia outfits, and 10 PUBG guns run 100% locally from device storage with zero internet latency required.
2. **🌐 Online 1v1 Socket.io Real-Time PvP (`node server.js`):**
   - When tapped inside `☰ MENU`, your phone connects via WebSockets to our included `server.js` (`port 3000 or production wss://`).
   - **Room Matchmaking (`find_match`):** Automatically pairs Phone A (`🔵 P1`) with Phone B (`🔴 P2`) in a private room (`Zonke-Room-1001`).
   - **60 FPS Trajectory Mirroring (`player_flick` -> `opponent_flick`):** When Player 1 flicks at $85\%$ power with $-1.4$ wind, the server relays that exact packet to Player 2 within $<30\text{ms}$. Both phones run the exact same `updatePhysics()` calculation simultaneously, mirroring the paper ball trajectory across screens!
3. **💥 Native Mobile Haptic Vibration (`@capacitor/haptics`):** Through `triggerHaptic(type)`, every wall ceiling impact, target hit ($🎯$), Tinfoil Revive ($🪙$), and gunshot elimination ($💀$) physically vibrates the user's mobile device!

  Receives live status heartbeats every 30 seconds from active clients (`Capacitor Native / Web Browser`), returning total active Socket.io connections and server status.

  Logs structured application events, login events, and alerts with exact severity levels (`info | warning | critical`):
  - **`APP_OPEN`:** Tracked on every client launch.
  - **`LOGIN_SUCCESS / LOGIN_FAILED`:** Tracked when user profiles are created or authenticated.

  Captures real-time **Core Web Vitals & Mobile Performance Metrics** from the app:
  - **`LCP` (Largest Contentful Paint):** Measured in milliseconds (`PerformanceObserver`).
  - **`INP` (Interaction to Next Paint):** Measured in milliseconds (`PerformanceObserver`).
  - **`CLS` (Cumulative Layout Shift):** Computed continuously across dynamic layout updates.
  - **`FPS` (Frames Per Second):** Monitored right inside `requestAnimationFrame(gameLoop)`.
- **`GET /api/app-detail/` (`Complete JSON Summary`)**:
  Returns a single unified JSON payload containing `lastHeartbeat`, `status`, metric counters (`appOpens, logins, failedLogins, errors, criticalAlerts`), and average Web Vitals for external dashboard scraping.
- **`GET /telemetry` (`Live Built-In Web Dashboard`)**:
  Visit `http://localhost:3000/telemetry` in your browser anytime to view a live, auto-refreshing dark-mode telemetry dashboard displaying exact metric counters, LCP/INP/CLS metrics, and recent event logs!
